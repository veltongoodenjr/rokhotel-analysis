Company profile

Brand foundations

ROK Hotel Kingston is a 168 room Hilton Tapestry Collection property positioned as a modern lifestyle hub in Downtown Kingston where business travel, art and local culture come together.

Core facts
  • Opened in 2022 as the first Hilton Tapestry Collection hotel in the Caribbean.
  • Located on the Kingston waterfront with panoramic city and harbour views.
  • 168 rooms and suites targeted at business and leisure guests.
  • Mix of corporate, meetings and weekend staycation segments.
Guest experience highlights
  • ROKstone rooftop bar and pool that position the hotel as a social meeting point in the city.
  • Multiple dining outlets including Palate and ROK Café plus curated food and beverage events.
  • Flexible meeting and event spaces aimed at corporate gatherings, launches and creatives.
  • Strong design language with Jamaican art pieces in public areas that support a local storytelling angle.
Urban lifestyle • Business plus leisure

Slide 4

Social media statistics and activity

Performance snapshot
Reach and scale

ROK has built a strong Instagram following since opening, placing the hotel ahead of several older Kingston competitors in audience size, although engagement per post is modest relative to that reach.

~27K Instagram followers
~3K Facebook followers
2–3 average posts per week on Instagram
  • Engagement on typical Instagram posts sits in the tens of likes and a small number of comments.
  • Video views for Reels show better reach but comments are still limited.
  • Audience mix suggests a blend of locals, Jamaican diaspora and international travellers researching Kingston.
Activity and content mix
  • Visual focus on rooms, pool deck, rooftop bar and food along with seasonal promotions.
  • Event pushes for brunches, themed nights and public holidays drive short bursts of content.
  • Little visible user generated content or guest stories on the grid.
  • Captions lean toward one way announcements instead of two way conversation with followers.
Takeaway: the hotel has strong visual assets and a healthy audience, but there is headroom to translate reach into deeper interaction and advocacy.

Slide 6

Key insights from analysis

What the data suggests
1. Reach outpaces engagement
  • ROK’s Instagram audience is large for a recent hotel, yet comment and share volumes are low.
  • This hints at a broad but passive follower base with room to nudge interaction.
2. Promotional bias
  • Many posts centre on offers, room types and events without much narrative around guest experience.
  • Competitors that balance sales with storytelling create a stronger emotional hook.
3. Under used community content
  • ROK shares little user generated content or staff stories on its main grid.
  • Hotels that spotlight guests, team members and local partners see richer engagement.
4. Platform gaps
  • Limited experiments with TikTok and no consistent X presence reduce visibility with younger and real time audiences.
  • Short form video trends in travel are a missed opportunity.
Slide 7

Recommendations to improve engagement

From reach to relationships
Content and storytelling
  • Shift from mostly promotional captions to short stories around guest moments, Kingston experiences and the feeling of staying at ROK.
  • Introduce regular staff and partner spotlights to humanise the brand and show the people behind the property.
  • Build a consistent user generated content pipeline by inviting guests to tag a clear hashtag and resharing the best posts.
Community and interaction
  • Use Stories for polls, questions and quick Q&A sessions for events, brunches and weekend stays.
  • Reply visibly to comments and DMs to reinforce that a real team is listening and responding.
  • Run simple social contests linked to on property rewards to stimulate shares and saves.
Platform strategy and measurement
  • Test a small series of TikTok or Reels concepts around rooftop vibes, food preparation and Kingston views.
  • Re establish a basic X presence for real time guest support and event coverage when relevant.
  • Track content performance by type (rooms, food, people, city) and double down on the formats that consistently earn saves, shares and comments.
Goal Turn followers into advocates
